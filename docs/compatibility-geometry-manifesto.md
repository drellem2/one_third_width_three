Compatibility Geometry Manifesto

The central object of mathematics is not the point, but the constraint.
A coherent world is not primary. It is an emergent completion of partial compatible information. Geometry, probability, and even classicality arise from the structure of compatibility among local freedoms.
The symmetric group provides the canonical model. A permutation is a maximally coherent ordering world. A poset is a sparse constraint system selecting admissible worlds inside this ambient noncommutative universe. Linear extensions are coherent completions. Adjacent swaps are local perturbations preserving compatibility.

The key observation is that incompatibility is expensive while compatibility proliferates combinatorially. Independent local freedoms generate dimensionality; dimensionality generates expansion; expansion generates balance, concentration, and emergent symmetry.

The 1/3–2/3 phenomenon is therefore not fundamentally combinatorial. It is geometric and spectral. A balanced pair is the macroscopic signature of surviving local commutativity inside a constrained noncommutative state space.

The primary research goal is to replace ad hoc combinatorics with an intrinsic theory of compatibility geometry.

Core principles:

1. Constraint systems are primary.
   Global coherent states are secondary.

2. Noncommutative systems are ambient universes of possible coherent worlds.
   Constraint objects carve out admissible regions.

3. Local commutative subalgebras represent independent compatible freedoms.
   These generate tangent directions, cubical structure, and effective dimension.

4. Expansion is the generic state of sufficiently rich compatibility geometries.
   Non-expansion signals dimensional collapse, product structure, or hidden rigidity.

5. Posets are the first canonical compatibility geometries.
   They are Galois-closed systems of order constraints on permutation space.

6. Spectral behavior is geometric behavior.
   Laplacians encode propagation through compatibility space; low conductance corresponds to rigid decomposition.

The first program is internal to the symmetric group.

Define:

* an intrinsic category of local commutative contexts inside C[S_n];
* a Galois correspondence between constraint systems and admissible regions;
* a local cubical geometry generated by commuting Coxeter moves;
* spectral invariants measuring compatibility expansion.

Then prove:

* rich local commutativity forces expansion;
* expansion forces balance;
* failure implies canonical low-dimensional decomposition.

The second program is abstraction.

  with
* intrinsic compatibility geometries associated functorially to noncommutative algebras.

Conjectural master principle:

Every sufficiently nondegenerate constrained noncommutative system exhibits emergent balance due to proliferation of independent compatible local freedoms.

The long-term goal is a bottom-up theory of geometry itself.

Not:
space with local irregularities,

but:
global structure emergent from sparse compatible constraints.

Classical worlds become extremal coherent sectors of deeper compatibility geometries. Expansion, entropy, and symmetry become consequences of local commutativity surviving inside large noncommutative universes.
