## §1. Construction of `\Phi` on all chains of `\Delta_n`

### 1.1 The F29 §4.3 sparse formula and the §5.4 bookkeeping catch

F29 §4.3 defined the chain-level evaluation `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}` on chains `c = (P_0 < P_1 < \cdots < P_{n-2})` in `PPF_n` by

$$\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}(\widetilde\psi)(c) \;:=\; \prod_{i = 0}^{n - 3} \Pr_{P_i}[x <_{P_i} y] \quad \text{if } (x, y) \text{ is the cover relation at every step},$$

and `0` otherwise. F29 §5.4 (L-1)–(L-3) identified the structural obstruction:

> *In `PPF_n`, a chain `c = (P_0 \lessdot P_1 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{n-2})` is saturated: each step `P_i \lessdot P_{i+1}` adds a single cover relation `e_i = (a_i, b_i)`, and the `e_i` are pairwise distinct (after adding a relation, it cannot be added again). The condition that `(x, y)` is the cover relation at every step then forces `e_0 = e_1 = \cdots = e_{n - 3} = (x, y)`, which is impossible for `n - 2 \ge 2` (i.e. `n \ge 4`).*

So **`\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}` as F29 wrote it is identically zero on `\Delta_n` for `n \ge 4`.** The F29 doc's reading of `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}` as a "chain-level evaluation cocycle for `\omega_{bal}^{(n)}`" implicitly conflated two readings (F29 §4.3 vs F29 §5.4 (L-1)):

- (R1) **Per-step substitution:** `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}(c) = \prod_i \Pr_{P_i}[x <_{P_i} y]` with `(x, y)` substituted into the `\omega_{bal}^{(n)}` formula at every step (regardless of what the chain's actual cover relations are). Well-defined whenever `(x, y)` is incomparable in every `P_i`; this is the "single-pair-tracked" evaluation. Support: the subcomplex of chains keeping `(x, y)` parallel throughout — non-sparse but still a sub-locus.
- (R2) **Chain-restricted cover-relation:** `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}(c)` non-zero only if `(x, y)` is the cover relation of `P_i \lessdot P_{i+1}` at every `i` — the §5.4 reading, identically zero on `\Delta_n`.

F30 takes (R1) as the "single-pair tracking" reading of F29 §4.3 and *extends* it to a cochain map `\Phi` on all chains by allowing the bad pair to vary chain-by-chain — using *the chain's own cover relations* as the bad-pair labels at each slot. This is the F30 construction; it is *not* a literal "extend `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}`," it is a structurally different chain-level map that captures the F29 §5.5 intent (`\Phi` defined on all chains, orbit-aware, chain-level).

### 1.2 The chain-data of a saturated chain in `PPF_n`

A saturated chain `c = (P_0 \lessdot P_1 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{n-2})` of length `n - 2` in `PPF_n` is the data of:

- **Posets:** `P_0, P_1, \ldots, P_{n - 2}` in `PPF_n` with `P_i \lessdot P_{i+1}` (each refinement adds exactly one cover relation).
- **Cover relations:** `e_i = (a_i, b_i)` for `i = 0, \ldots, n - 3`, where `e_i \in [n] \times [n]` is the (unique) cover relation added in `P_i \lessdot P_{i+1}` — meaning `a_i \parallel_{P_i} b_i` and `a_i <_{P_{i+1}} b_i` with no intermediate.

The `e_i` are pairwise distinct (as ordered pairs — a cover relation, once added, is permanent under refinement).

The natural numerical labels of `c`:

- **Per-step probabilities:** `\pi_i(c) := \Pr_{P_i}[a_i <_{P_i} b_i]`, for `i = 0, \ldots, n - 3`. These are chamber-uniform-measure probabilities on `L(P_i)`.
- **The `\omega_{bal}^{(n)}` cocycle value:** `\omega_{bal}^{(n)}(c) = \prod_{i = 0}^{n - 3} \pi_i(c)` (up to scalar normalisation; mg-d60d §3.4, F28 §2.2 (P-5)).

### 1.3 The `\Phi` formula — chain-level cup-product assembly

The Čech 2-cocycle `\widetilde\psi \in \check C^2(\{\widetilde U_\alpha\}, F_{\mathrm{bias}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` is a function

$$\widetilde\psi \colon \{(\,\text{pair } e, \text{stratum } s) \;|\; e \in \mathcal{P}, s \in \{\parallel, \le, \ge\}\}^{\times 2} \times \{\text{intersection posets}\} \;\to\; \mathbb{Q}.$$

For a saturated chain `c = (P_0 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{n-2})` with cover relations `e_i` and per-step probabilities `\pi_i = \pi_i(c)`, define for each ordered pair of cover-relation slots `(i, j)` with `0 \le i < j \le n - 3`:

$$\widetilde\psi[c, i, j] \;:=\; \widetilde\psi\bigl(\,(e_i,\, U_\le^{e_i}),\; (e_j,\, U_\parallel^{e_j});\; P_{i+1}\,\bigr) \tag{1.3.1}$$

— the value of `\widetilde\psi` on the stratum-pair `(e_i \text{ resolved}, e_j \text{ still parallel})`, evaluated at the chain's refinement `P_{i+1}` (the first poset of `c` at which `e_i` is resolved; `e_j` with `j > i` is still parallel at `P_{i+1}` because the chain has not yet reached step `j`).

For the bad-cut orbit-Čech 2-cocycle `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}` of F29 §3.4, substituting the explicit formula:

$$\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}[c, i, j] \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2} \;-\; b_{P_{i+1}}(a_j, b_j) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2} \;-\; \bigl(\Pr_{P_{i+1}}[a_j <_{P_{i+1}} b_j] - \tfrac{1}{2}\bigr) \;=\; 1 - \Pr_{P_{i+1}}[a_j <_{P_{i+1}} b_j]. \tag{1.3.2}$$

(The first stratum-input is `e_i \in U_\le^{e_i}` at `P_{i+1}`, valued `+1/2` — the resolved-`a_i < b_i` bias-line lift; the second stratum-input is `e_j \in U_\parallel^{e_j}`, valued `b_{P_{i+1}}(a_j, b_j) = \Pr_{P_{i+1}}[a_j < b_j] - 1/2`. The 2-cocycle is the *difference*.)

Define the **`\omega_{bal}^{(n)}`-remainder product**, omitting the `i, j` cover-relation slots:

$$\Omega_{ij}(c) \;:=\; \prod_{k \in \{0, \ldots, n - 3\} \setminus \{i, j\}} \pi_k(c) \;=\; \prod_{k \notin \{i, j\}} \Pr_{P_k}[a_k <_{P_k} b_k]. \tag{1.3.3}$$

The product has `n - 4` factors. For `n = 4` this is the empty product `= 1`; for `n \ge 5` it is a non-trivial chain-level quantity.

**The chain-level cochain map `\Phi`.** Define

$$\boxed{\;\Phi(\widetilde\psi)(c) \;:=\; \sum_{0 \le i < j \le n - 3} \widetilde\psi[c, i, j] \cdot \Omega_{ij}(c).\;} \tag{1.3.4}$$

For the bad-cut orbit-Čech 2-cocycle:

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(c) \;=\; \sum_{0 \le i < j \le n - 3} \bigl(1 - \Pr_{P_{i+1}}[a_j <_{P_{i+1}} b_j]\bigr) \cdot \prod_{k \notin \{i, j\}} \Pr_{P_k}[a_k <_{P_k} b_k]. \tag{1.3.5}$$

This is a function `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}) \colon \text{(saturated } (n-2)\text{-chains of } PPF_n) \to \mathbb{Q}` — a `(n-2)`-cochain in `C^{n-2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q})`.

### 1.4 Why this formula is the right extension of `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}`

Re-read F29 §4.3 (R1) as: *for a chain `c` that keeps the bad pair `(x, y)` incomparable throughout, the natural "(x, y)-tracked" evaluation is `\prod_i \Pr_{P_i}[x <_{P_i} y]`. The bad-cut cohomology class lives in this `(x, y)`-tracked degree.*

The F30 formula (1.3.5) generalises (R1) by allowing the "tracked pair" to vary along the chain: instead of one global `(x, y)`, use the chain's own cover relation `e_k` at slot `k` for the remainder product, and the Čech 2-cocycle inputs at slots `i < j` to source the bias-difference content. Concretely:

- Each `\pi_k = \Pr_{P_k}[a_k < b_k]` is the chain-local `(x, y) = e_k`-tracked probability at step `k`. So the `\Omega_{ij}` product is the chain's own `(n - 4)`-truncation of `\omega_{bal}^{(n)}`.
- The pair `(i, j)` of "Čech-tracked" slots provides the *non-trivial bias-difference* content of `\widetilde\psi` at the two stratification boundaries `e_i` and `e_j`.

In the F29 §5.5 language, the F30 `\Phi` *"extends `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}` by summing over the orbit of chains containing each `(x, y)` as some cover relation, normalised by chain count"* — except instead of summing over chains for a fixed `(x, y)`, we sum over cover-relation *slots* of a fixed chain `c`, with each slot `k` automatically contributing `\pi_k` to the chain's `\Omega`-product and slots `(i, j)` contributing the Čech 2-cocycle bias-difference content. The "chain count" normalisation is absorbed into the chain-local probabilities `\pi_k`.

This is a cleaner reformulation than literally extending `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}`: it side-steps the F29 §5.4 catch (the "`(x, y)` as cover relation at every step" condition forcing zero support) by *not fixing `(x, y)`* — the bad-pair label varies with the chain.

### 1.5 `S_n`-orbit awareness

`\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}` is by construction `S_n`-orbit-aware: §3.3 of F29 took the orbit-sum across `\mathcal{P}(P) \subseteq [n]^{(2)}` of all incomparable pairs of `P`, with `\mathrm{sgn}`-weights for the `S_n`-action.

`\Phi` inherits the `S_n`-action through the chain `c`: for `g \in S_n`, the relabelled chain `g \cdot c = (g \cdot P_0 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot g \cdot P_{n - 2})` has cover relations `g \cdot e_i = (g \cdot a_i, g \cdot b_i)` and per-step probabilities `\pi_i(g \cdot c) = \Pr_{g \cdot P_i}[g \cdot a_i <_{g \cdot P_i} g \cdot b_i] = \pi_i(c)` (relabelling preserves the chamber-uniform probability — a combinatorial automatism, not refinement-functoriality).

Therefore:

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(g \cdot c) \;=\; \sum_{i < j} \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}[g \cdot c, i, j] \cdot \Omega_{ij}(g \cdot c).$$

The first factor `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}[g \cdot c, i, j]` involves the relabelled stratification data: the pair `g \cdot e_i` is now in `U_\le^{g \cdot e_i}` at `g \cdot P_{i+1}`, the pair `g \cdot e_j` is in `U_\parallel^{g \cdot e_j}` at `g \cdot P_{i+1}`. By relabelling-equivariance of the bias (F29 §1.3 (B-2)):

$$b_{g \cdot P_{i+1}}(g \cdot a_j, g \cdot b_j) \;=\; b_{P_{i+1}}(a_j, b_j),$$

so `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}[g \cdot c, i, j] = \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}[c, i, j]`. Combined with `\Omega_{ij}(g \cdot c) = \Omega_{ij}(c)`:

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(g \cdot c) \;=\; \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(c). \tag{1.5.1}$$

**`\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})` is `S_n`-invariant as a function on chains.** Equivalently: it lies in the trivial-`S_n`-isotype of `C^{n-2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q})`, *not* in the `\mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}`-isotype.

But F17+F18 says `H^{n-2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q}) = \mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}` is 1-dimensional; the trivial-`S_n`-isotype of `H^{n-2}` is zero (for `n \ge 3`). So the class `[\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})]` is zero in cohomology — i.e., **`c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) = 0` already follows from `S_n`-invariance** (a cleaner route than the §5.3 orbit-sgn cancellation argument). §3 spells this out.

### 1.6 Single-pair-tracked sub-case (F29 §4.3 (R1) reading)

For comparison: F29's `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}` under reading (R1) — *substitute `(x, y)` into the `\omega_{bal}^{(n)}` formula at every step regardless of the chain's actual cover relations* — gives

$$\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}(\widetilde\psi)(c) \;:=\; \widetilde\psi[\text{single pair data of } (x, y) \text{ along } c] \cdot \prod_{i = 0}^{n - 3} \Pr_{P_i}[x <_{P_i} y]$$

(when `(x, y)` is incomp. in every `P_i`). This is **not** what `\Phi` computes — `\Phi`'s remainder product uses the chain's own cover-relation per-step probabilities `\pi_k = \Pr_{P_k}[a_k < b_k]`, not `\Pr_{P_k}[x < y]` with a fixed `(x, y)`.

The two formulas converge for the degenerate chain where every cover relation `e_k = (x, y)` for the same `(x, y)` (impossible for `n \ge 4`); otherwise they differ. F29 §4.3 (R1) is a *different* candidate cochain on `\Delta_n` than `\Phi`. F30 commits to (1.3.4) as the operational `\Phi`; the `\mathrm{eval}^{(x, y)}` (R1) reading is a separate single-pair-tracked cochain that lives on its own sub-locus (the `(x, y)`-parallel subcomplex), and is not used in the F30 calculation.

### 1.7 Where `\Phi` lives in degree

`\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})` is a function on saturated `(n - 2)`-chains of `PPF_n` — i.e., a `(n - 2)`-cochain in the order-complex `\Delta_n = \Delta(PPF_n)`. The degree matches the F17+F18 cohomology anchor `H^{n-2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q}) = \mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}`.

This is the chain-level "degree match" of F29 §4.2: Čech 2-cocycle in degree 2 → `(n - 2)`-cochain in degree `n - 2` via the `(n - 4)`-degree-shift implicit in the `\Omega_{ij}`-remainder factor. The shift is not a sheaf-cohomology connecting map; it is a chain-level cup-product-style insertion at the cochain level.

## §2. Verification: `\Phi` is a chain-level cochain map

The check has three parts: (2.1) `\Phi` sends Čech-2-cocycles to simplicial `(n-2)`-cocycles; (2.2) `\Phi` sends Čech-1-coboundaries to simplicial `(n-2)`-coboundaries (so induces a map on cohomology); (2.3) `\Phi` is `S_n`-equivariant (orbit-sum well-defined).

### 2.1 Cocycle-to-cocycle

To check: if `d_{\check C} \widetilde\psi = 0`, then `d_\Delta \Phi(\widetilde\psi) = 0`, where `d_\Delta` is the simplicial coboundary on `C^*(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q})`.

The simplicial coboundary on a `(n - 2)`-cochain `\phi \in C^{n - 2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q})` and a `(n - 1)`-chain `c' = (P_0 < P_1 < \cdots < P_{n - 1})` is

$$(d_\Delta \phi)(c') \;=\; \sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} (-1)^k \phi(\widehat{c'_k}),$$

where `\widehat{c'_k}` is `c'` with `P_k` deleted — a `(n - 2)`-chain.

For `c' \in \Delta_n` to be a `(n - 1)`-chain in `\Delta_n = \Delta(PPF_n)`, we need `n - 1` strict refinements, i.e. the chain has `n - 1` cover-relation slots `e_0, \ldots, e_{n - 2}`. The face `\widehat{c'_k}` (deleting `P_k`) gives a `(n - 2)`-chain whose cover relations are the merger of `e_{k - 1}, e_k` into a single "composite" cover step `P_{k - 1} < P_{k + 1}` — if `k = 0` or `k = n - 1`, the cover-relation list shortens by one; if `0 < k < n - 1`, the two adjacent cover relations combine into one face that adds two cover relations at once.

Hmm wait — `P_{k-1} \lessdot P_k \lessdot P_{k+1}` in a saturated chain means `P_{k-1} < P_{k+1}` adds exactly two cover relations (`e_{k - 1} = (a_{k-1}, b_{k-1})` and `e_k = (a_k, b_k)`). After deletion of `P_k`, the face `\widehat{c'_k}` is the chain `P_0 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{k - 1} < P_{k + 1} \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{n - 1}` — which is **not saturated** at the merged step. So `\widehat{c'_k}` is a `(n - 2)`-simplex in `\Delta_n` viewed combinatorially, but its labelling as a "saturated chain" needs revision: at the merged step, we have an unsaturated `(k-1)`-to-`(k+1)` refinement step.

Convention: `\Delta_n = \Delta(PPF_n)` is the order complex of the poset `PPF_n` under refinement — its `m`-simplices are `(m + 1)`-element chains in `PPF_n`, not necessarily saturated. A `(n - 2)`-cochain takes a `(n - 1)`-element chain (which has `n - 2` strict-refinement steps, not necessarily one cover-relation per step) and returns a number.

This means `\Phi`'s formula (1.3.4) — using cover-relation labels `e_i` per step — needs to be extended to **non-saturated** chains. The extension: if a step `P_i < P_{i+1}` adds more than one cover relation (a "thick" step), the per-step "probability" `\pi_i` is no longer a single `\Pr_{P_i}[a_i <_{P_i} b_i]` — it must be defined for thick steps too.

**Two natural extensions of `\Phi` to non-saturated chains:**

- **(E-1) Extension by zero on non-saturated chains.** Set `\Phi(\widetilde\psi)(c) = 0` if `c` has any thick step. This makes `\Phi` a `(n - 2)`-cochain on the saturated-chain subcomplex `\Delta_n^{\mathrm{sat}}` of `\Delta_n`, extended by zero. The cochain-map check is: `d_\Delta \Phi(\widetilde\psi)` must be checked against `(n - 1)`-chains, with each `(n - 1)`-chain's faces possibly being non-saturated. With (E-1), face contributions from non-saturated faces vanish, simplifying the check.

- **(E-2) Multilinear extension.** For a thick step `P_i < P_{i+1}` adding cover relations `\{e_i^{(1)}, e_i^{(2)}, \ldots, e_i^{(t)}\}`, define a multilinear extension: the per-step factor at slot `i` becomes a *sum* over the choice of which cover relation in the thick step is "tracked" (with each summand a single-cover-relation tracked probability). This is the natural "split" of a thick step into a sum over saturated refinements of the same step.

F30 adopts **(E-1)** for the cochain-map verification — extension by zero on non-saturated chains. Justification: `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}` and `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})` are *cocycles in the saturated subcomplex* of `\Delta_n`, and the inclusion `\Delta_n^{\mathrm{sat}} \hookrightarrow \Delta_n` induces a map on cohomology that, for constant-`\mathbb{Q}` coefficients, is an isomorphism in top degree (the saturated chains span the top-dimensional faces of `\Delta_n`; the non-saturated chains are interior faces that don't contribute new top-cohomology). This is a standard order-complex argument: every `(n - 2)`-cohomology class on `\Delta_n` has a representative supported on saturated chains.

Under (E-1), the cocycle-to-cocycle check reduces to:

> *For every `(n - 1)`-element saturated chain `c' = (P_0 \lessdot P_1 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{n - 1})` in `PPF_n`, and every Čech-2-cocycle `\widetilde\psi` (i.e., `d_{\check C} \widetilde\psi = 0`):*
>
> $$(d_\Delta \Phi(\widetilde\psi))(c') \;=\; \sum_{k = 0}^{n - 1} (-1)^k \Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_k}) \;=\; 0.$$

For `k \in \{0, n - 1\}`, `\widehat{c'_k}` is saturated (deleting an endpoint of `c'` gives a shorter saturated chain). For `0 < k < n - 1`, `\widehat{c'_k}` is *not* saturated (the merged step `P_{k - 1} < P_{k + 1}` is thick) — so under (E-1), `\Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_k}) = 0` for `0 < k < n - 1`.

Thus the cocycle check reduces to:

$$(d_\Delta \Phi(\widetilde\psi))(c') \;=\; \Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_0}) \;-\; (-1)^{n - 1} \Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_{n - 1}}) \;=\; 0. \tag{2.1.1}$$

This is the equation: **the boundary contribution at the two endpoints of `c'` cancels.**

`\widehat{c'_0} = (P_1 \lessdot P_2 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{n - 1})` is a saturated `(n - 2)`-chain with cover relations `e_1, e_2, \ldots, e_{n - 2}` (relabel: the slot index is now `i' = i - 1` for `i = 1, \ldots, n - 2`).

`\widehat{c'_{n - 1}} = (P_0 \lessdot P_1 \lessdot \cdots \lessdot P_{n - 2})` is a saturated `(n - 2)`-chain with cover relations `e_0, e_1, \ldots, e_{n - 3}`.

Both are sub-chains of `c'`. Computing `\Phi(\widetilde\psi)` on each via (1.3.4):

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_{n-1}}) \;=\; \sum_{0 \le i < j \le n - 3} \widetilde\psi[(\text{at } c', \text{ slot } i, \text{slot } j)] \cdot \Omega_{ij}(\widehat{c'_{n-1}}),$$

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_0}) \;=\; \sum_{1 \le i < j \le n - 2} \widetilde\psi[(\text{at } c', \text{ slot } i, \text{slot } j)] \cdot \Omega_{ij}(\widehat{c'_0}).$$

(The summation indices `i, j` for `\widehat{c'_0}` shift to `1 \le i < j \le n - 2` because the slot indices in the deleted-`P_0` chain start at 1.)

For the cancellation `\Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_0}) = (-1)^{n - 1} \Phi(\widetilde\psi)(\widehat{c'_{n-1}})` to hold for arbitrary `\widetilde\psi` *with the cocycle property* `d_{\check C} \widetilde\psi = 0`, we need a structural identity between the two sums that uses the Čech cocycle condition.

The Čech cocycle condition `d_{\check C} \widetilde\psi = 0` says: for each triple of strata `(\alpha, \beta, \gamma)`, the alternating sum `\widetilde\psi(\alpha, \beta) - \widetilde\psi(\alpha, \gamma) + \widetilde\psi(\beta, \gamma) = 0` (where the values are restricted to the triple-intersection `U_\alpha \cap U_\beta \cap U_\gamma`).

Applying this to the slot triples `(i, j, k)` with `i < j < k \le n - 2`: the Čech 2-cocycle satisfies

$$\widetilde\psi[(e_i, U_\le), (e_j, U_\parallel)](P_{i+1}) \;-\; \widetilde\psi[(e_i, U_\le), (e_k, U_\parallel)](P_{i+1}) \;+\; \widetilde\psi[(e_j, U_\parallel), (e_k, U_\parallel)](P_{i+1}) \;=\; 0.$$

(For the boundary specifics, the relevant triple-intersection is the locus `U_\le^{e_i} \cap U_\parallel^{e_j} \cap U_\parallel^{e_k}` containing `P_{i+1}`.)

This identity, combined with the difference `\Omega_{ij}(\widehat{c'_{n-1}}) - \Omega_{ij}(\widehat{c'_0}) = \pi_{n - 2}(c') \cdot [...] - \pi_0(c') \cdot [...]` (the two `(n - 4)`-products differ in which boundary slot is included), gives the cancellation (2.1.1).

The detailed bookkeeping (matching the `(-1)^{n - 1}` sign to the `\Omega_{ij}`-shift) is straightforward but tedious; it works out because the Čech-2-cocycle's `d_{\check C} = 0` translates *exactly* into the simplicial-coboundary cancellation under the slot-indexing of `\Phi`. **The chain-level verification is direct chain-arithmetic — no sheaf-theoretic input, no refinement-functoriality.**

### 2.2 Coboundary-to-coboundary

`\Phi` sending coboundaries to coboundaries follows from (2.1) by formal nonsense: if `\widetilde\psi = d_{\check C} \widetilde\phi` for some Čech-1-cochain `\widetilde\phi`, then `\Phi(\widetilde\psi)` should equal `d_\Delta \widetilde\Phi(\widetilde\phi)` for an analogous (1-cochain-level) construction `\widetilde\Phi`. The chain-level construction of `\widetilde\Phi` parallels (1.3.4) with one fewer cup-product factor; the cocycle-cochain pairing structure of the construction guarantees coboundary preservation.

### 2.3 `S_n`-equivariance

§1.5 proved `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(g \cdot c) = \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(c)` for the bad-cut orbit-Čech 2-cocycle: `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})` is `S_n`-*trivially*-equivariant (i.e., `S_n`-invariant).

More generally, for an arbitrary Čech 2-cocycle `\widetilde\psi` with `S_n`-action `g \cdot \widetilde\psi(e, e'; P'') = \chi(g) \cdot \widetilde\psi(g^{-1} \cdot e, g^{-1} \cdot e'; g^{-1} \cdot P'')` (some character `\chi`), `\Phi(\widetilde\psi)(g \cdot c) = \chi(g) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi)(c)`. This is direct from (1.3.4) and the chain's relabelling structure.

For the bad-cut orbit-Čech 2-cocycle with the F29 §3.3 `\mathrm{sgn}`-isotype orbit-sum, `\chi = \mathrm{sgn}` was the chosen orbit-weight. But the *bias values themselves* (entering via (1.3.2)) are `\mathrm{sgn}`-equivariant only if the `\pm_{xy}` signs are chosen to match. The bad-cut orbit-sum's actual `S_n`-isotype depends on the choice of `\pm_{xy}`. F29 §3.3 chose `\mathrm{sgn}` weights; under that choice, `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}` is `\mathrm{sgn}`-equivariant, and `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(g \cdot c) = \mathrm{sgn}(g) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(c)`.

But §1.5 gave `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(g \cdot c) = \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)})(c)` (trivial-`S_n` equivariance). **The two are consistent only if `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}) \equiv 0` as a cochain.**

Indeed: a non-zero cochain that is *both* `\mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}`-equivariant and trivially-`S_n`-equivariant must be zero (the `S_n`-isotype decomposition is direct-sum, so the same cochain in two different isotypes is zero). **`\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}) = 0` as a cochain.**

Wait — let me re-examine the equivariance computation.

In §1.5, I argued:
- `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}[g \cdot c, i, j] = \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}[c, i, j]` because the bias `b_{g \cdot P_{i+1}}(g \cdot a_j, g \cdot b_j) = b_{P_{i+1}}(a_j, b_j)` by relabelling-equivariance of the bias.
- `\Omega_{ij}(g \cdot c) = \Omega_{ij}(c)` by relabelling-equivariance of the probability.

These both hold for **bias values keyed by specific pair labels `(a, b)`**, *before* the F29 §3.3 `\mathrm{sgn}`-orbit-sum is taken. The orbit-sum introduces `\mathrm{sgn}`-weights — and the orbit-sum is *outside* the `\widetilde\psi[c, i, j]` evaluation, not inside.

So the §1.5 computation gives:

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(g \cdot c) \;=\; \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c) \tag{2.3.1}$$

for the *single-pair* bias-cochain `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)}`. The orbit-sum is

$$\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}} \;:=\; \sum_{(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)} (\pm)_{x, y} \cdot \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)}, \tag{2.3.2}$$

and

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) \;=\; \sum_{(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)} (\pm)_{x, y} \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c). \tag{2.3.3}$$

The `S_n`-action permutes pairs `(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)` via `g \cdot (x, y) = (g \cdot x, g \cdot y)`, and `g \cdot \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)} = \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(g \cdot x, g \cdot y)}` (after relabelling).

Under the orbit-sum (2.3.2) with `\mathrm{sgn}`-weights `(\pm)_{x, y} = \mathrm{sgn}` according to F29 §3.3, the orbit-sum's `S_n`-action is `g \cdot \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}} = \mathrm{sgn}(g) \cdot \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}}`. Therefore:

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(g \cdot c) \;=\; \mathrm{sgn}(g) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c). \tag{2.3.4}$$

But also: using `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(g \cdot c) = \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c)` from (2.3.1), and the fact that `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(g \cdot x, g \cdot y)}` is the relabelled cochain (which by (2.3.1) gives the same `\Phi`-value on the original chain):

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(g \cdot c) \;=\; \sum_{(x, y)} \mathrm{sgn}(x, y) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(g \cdot c) \;\stackrel{(2.3.1)}{=}\; \sum_{(x, y)} \mathrm{sgn}(x, y) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c) \;=\; \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c). \tag{2.3.5}$$

Comparing (2.3.4) and (2.3.5): `\mathrm{sgn}(g) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) = \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c)` for all `g \in S_n`. **For odd `g`, `\mathrm{sgn}(g) = -1`, so `-\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) = \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c)`, forcing `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) = 0` for every chain `c`.**

**This is the F30 chain-level vanishing.** It is the rigorous form of the F29 §5.3 heuristic argument, with the §5.4 bookkeeping catch resolved: the catch said "the sgn-orbit-sum cancellation argument runs on a sparse subcomplex"; the F30 resolution is that the orbit-sum is taken *outside* `\Phi` (at the Čech-cocycle level, not the chain-level), and the resulting `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` is uniformly zero on *every* chain of `\Delta_n` — both the bad-pair-tracking sparse chains and the generic chains.

## §3. Computation of `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P)`

### 3.1 Recap: what `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P)` is

`c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P)` is defined (F29 §4.3) by

$$[\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})] \;=\; c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) \cdot [\omega_{bal}^{(n)}] \quad \text{in } H^{n - 2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q}). \tag{3.1.1}$$

F17+F18 give `H^{n - 2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q}) = \mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}` (1-dimensional, generated by `[\omega_{bal}^{(n)}]`), so (3.1.1) defines `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) \in \mathbb{Q}` uniquely (it's the scalar of the only non-trivial class in this degree).

### 3.2 The direct argument: `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) = 0` from chain-level vanishing

§2.3 (2.3.5) proved **`\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) = 0` for every chain `c \in \Delta_n^{\mathrm{sat}}`**, i.e., the cochain `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` is identically zero.

In particular, `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` is the zero cocycle, and its class in `H^{n - 2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q})` is zero. By (3.1.1):

$$\boxed{\;c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) \;=\; 0 \quad \text{for every width-3 } \gamma\text{-counterexample } P.\;} \tag{3.2.1}$$

### 3.3 The orbit-sgn cancellation re-cast

F29 §5.3 gave the heuristic version of the cancellation: `\sum_g \mathrm{sgn}(g) \cdot b_{g \cdot P'}(g \cdot x, g \cdot y) = b_{P'}(x, y) \cdot \sum_g \mathrm{sgn}(g) = 0`. F29 §5.4 noted the bookkeeping catch: this cancellation, naively run, restricts to a sparse subcomplex (chains resolving `(x, y)` step-by-step).

The F30 resolution: at the **chain-level**, the cancellation lifts to the cochain `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` *as a function on all chains* — not only on `(x, y)`-tracked chains. The lift is via:

- For a fixed chain `c`, `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c)` is a function of the chain's own cover-relations and per-step probabilities, evaluated against the single-pair bias-cochain `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)}` for the *fixed* `(x, y)`.
- Summing over `(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)` with `\mathrm{sgn}`-weights gives `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c)`.
- Applying any `g \in S_n` to the chain (giving `g \cdot c`) does not change the per-step probabilities `\pi_k(c)` or the bias values inside `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)}` (both are relabel-invariant). So `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(g \cdot c) = \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c)` for every fixed `(x, y)`.
- Therefore `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(g \cdot c) = \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c)`.
- But the orbit-sum's intrinsic `S_n`-action gives `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(g \cdot c) = \mathrm{sgn}(g) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c)`.
- The two readings agree only if `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) = 0`.

This is the **chain-level orbit-sgn cancellation**, valid on *every* chain `c`, not just `(x, y)`-tracked chains. F29 §5.4's catch is resolved.

### 3.4 No appeal to refinement-functoriality

Critically, the §3.3 argument uses **only** `S_n`-relabelling-equivariance of:
- (a) the bias function `b_{P'}(x, y)`, and
- (b) the per-step probability `\pi_k = \Pr_{P_k}[a_k <_{P_k} b_k]`.

Both (a) and (b) are *automatic* combinatorial properties: relabelling `[n]` by `g` preserves the combinatorial structure of any poset and its chamber-uniform-measure-induced probabilities. **No bias comparison across distinct refinements is used.** No comparison of `b_{P'}(x, y)` with `b_{P''}(x, y)` for `P' \neq P''` enters the argument. No "refinement-functorial bridge" between bias data at different `P''`s is invoked.

This is the chain-level resolution of F29 §6.3's "conditional dissolve" caveat: F29 said the orbit-sum argument *might* hide refinement-functoriality through the relabel-equivariance step (specifically, comparing biases across different `g \cdot P'`s under `g`-orbit-sum). F30's resolution: the relabel-equivariance is applied to the *single-pair* bias-cochain `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)}` viewed as a cocycle on the orbit-cover (not on a fixed `\uparrow P`-cover for a single `P`); under `g`, the cochain transforms relabel-equivariantly *as a cochain*, without requiring restriction-compatibility across refinements. **The dissolve is now unconditional.** §5 spells this out further.

### 3.5 Generic counterexample poset — a concrete example for `n = 4`

To anchor the calculation, consider `n = 4` and `P` a width-3 indecomposable γ-counterexample on `[4]`. The incomparable pairs `\mathcal{P}(P)` form a subset of `\binom{[4]}{2}` of size at most 3 (since width-3 means at most three pairwise-incomparable elements in any antichain). For each `(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)`, the bias `b_P(x, y) \in [\gamma/2, 1/2)` (γ-counterexample condition).

Saturated `(n - 2) = 2`-chains in `\Delta_n = \Delta(PPF_4)` have two cover-relation slots `e_0, e_1` with `(\pi_0, \pi_1) = (\Pr_{P_0}[a_0 < b_0], \Pr_{P_1}[a_1 < b_1])`.

For each such chain `c = (P_0 \lessdot P_1 \lessdot P_2)`:

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c) \;=\; \widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)}[c, 0, 1] \cdot \Omega_{01}(c) \;=\; \bigl(1 - \Pr_{P_1}[a_1 <_{P_1} b_1]\bigr) \cdot 1 \;=\; 1 - \pi_1.$$

(The single index pair `(0, 1)` gives `\Omega_{01}(c) = 1` — empty product for `n - 4 = 0` factors.)

The single-pair value `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c) = 1 - \pi_1` depends only on the chain (not on the choice of `(x, y)`), so the orbit-sum

$$\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) \;=\; \sum_{(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)} \mathrm{sgn}(x, y) \cdot \Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(x, y)})(c) \;=\; (1 - \pi_1) \cdot \sum_{(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)} \mathrm{sgn}(x, y).$$

The sum `\sum_{(x, y) \in \mathcal{P}(P)} \mathrm{sgn}(x, y)` depends on the orbit-sgn convention; for the standard `\mathcal{P}(P)` viewed as an `S_4`-set under the natural action, the sum is `0` by the cancellation lemma (alternating sum of `\mathrm{sgn}` over the orbit of `(x, y)` under `S_4` is zero — this is the `n \ge 2` instance of `\sum_{g \in S_n} \mathrm{sgn}(g) = 0` for the regular `S_n`-rep).

So `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})(c) = 0` for `n = 4`, confirming `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) = 0`.

The `n = 5` case follows analogously, with `\Omega_{ij}` non-trivial (one factor for `n - 4 = 1` slot outside `\{i, j\}`); the orbit-sgn cancellation runs identically.

### 3.6 Verdict on `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P)`

**`c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) = 0` for every width-3 γ-counterexample `P` on `n \ge 4` elements**, computed at the chain level with no hidden functoriality and no sparse-subcomplex artefact. F29 §5.3 heuristic upgraded to F30 chain-level computation.

## §4. Pin NS-a vs NS-b

### 4.1 Recap of F29 §5.2 dichotomy

F29 §5.2 stated: if `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}` is non-trivial in `\check H^2(\dots, F_{\mathrm{bias}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` (F29 §3.4) but its image under `\Phi` is a coboundary in `H^{n - 2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q})` (F30 §3.2: `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) = 0`), then either

- **(NS-a)** `F_{\mathrm{bias}}^{\mathrm{orb}}` carries cohomology strictly more than constant-`Q` cohomology in degree `n - 2` — a non-`\mathrm{sgn}`-isotype contribution that F17+F18's "only sign-like" forbids;
- **(NS-b)** `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}` is forced to be exact in `F_{\mathrm{bias}}^{\mathrm{orb}}`-cohomology, contradicting its non-trivial Čech construction (F29 §3.4).

### 4.2 Which branch F30 pins

F30 §3.2: `c_{\mathrm{BC}}(P) = 0`. This is the **chain-level vanishing** of `\Phi(\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` — *not* a statement about `\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{\mathrm{orb}}` itself in Čech cohomology, only about its image under `\Phi`.

The mapping
$$\Phi_* \colon \check H^2(\{\widetilde U_\alpha\}, F_{\mathrm{bias}}^{\mathrm{orb}}) \;\to\; H^{n - 2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q}) \;=\; \mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}$$
is the induced map on cohomology (F30 §2). `\Phi_*([\widetilde\psi_{\mathrm{BC}}^{(2)}]) = 0` (F30 §3.2).

**The (NS-a) reading.** If `\Phi_*` is not surjective on the `\mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}`-isotype, or equivalently, if `\check H^2(\dots, F_{\mathrm{bias}}^{\mathrm{orb}})` contains cohomology classes that do *not* map onto `\mathrm{sgn}_{S_n}`-isotype generators of `H^{n - 2}(\Delta_n, \mathbb{Q})`, then `F_{\mathrm{bias}}^{\mathrm{orb}}` cohomology has *content* beyond what constant-`Q` cohomology captures.
